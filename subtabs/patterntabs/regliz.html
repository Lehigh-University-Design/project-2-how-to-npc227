Step 1

Cut a length of elastic string roughly double the length of your arm (~4 ft). Place one green bead on the left string. Thread the right string through in the opposite direction. Then, place the ends of both string in one hand, and use the other hand to pull the bead towards yourself. Once the bead is centered on the string, tie a knot using the string's ends to keep the bead in place.

Step 2

Place 2 green beads on the left string. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the initial bead.

Step 3

Eye time! Take the left string, place one blue bead, one green, and one more blue bead on it. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the previous row.

Step 4

Place 3 green beads on the left string. Then place 3 orange beads on the left string. thread the left string around the orange beads and back through the green beads. Hold the green beads with your left hand, and pull on the tail end of the left string using your right hand until the leg is flush with the lizard's body. Repeat this for the right string to add another leg.

Step 5

Place 2 green beads on the left string. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the initial bead.

Step 6

Place one green, one pink, and another green bead on the left string. Thread the right string through the green, pink, green beads headed the opposite way. Grab both ends and pull in opposite directions.

Repeat this step two more times, adding two pink beads instead of one. Then, do one final row with only one pink bead. This completes the lizard's body.

Step 7

Place 3 green beads on the right string. Then place 3 orange beads on the right string. thread the right string around the orange beads and back through the green beads. Hold the green beads with your right hand, and pull on the tail end of the right string using your left hand until the leg is flush with the lizard's body. Repeat this for the left string to add another leg.

Step 8

Place a singular green bead on the left string. Thread the right string through going the bead heading the opposite way as the left string's tail end. Pull in opposite directions. Repeat this a minimum of 2 times to create the tail of your lizard.

Step 9

Place a singular pink bead on the left string. Thread the right string through going the bead heading the opposite way as the left string's tail end. Pull in opposite directions. Repeat this a minimum of 2 times to embellish your lizard's tail!

All Done!

You are all done! Congrats on finishing the pattern. Now that you've finished, your bead lizard should look like the one on the right. If you've gotten the pattern down expertly, you can always try to make it again in a different color or try a harder pattern!
