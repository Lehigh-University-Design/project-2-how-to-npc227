Stegosaurus

Step 1

Cut a length of elastic string roughly 5 feet. Place one green bead on the left string. Thread the right string through in the opposite direction. Then, place the ends of both string in one hand, and use the other hand to pull the bead towards yourself. Once the bead is centered on the string, tie a knot using the string's ends to keep the bead in place.

Step 2

Place 2 green beads on the left string. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the initial bead.

Step 3

Eye time! Take the left string, place one green bead, one purple bead, and two more green beads on it. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the previous row.

Step 4

Place 3 green beads on the left string. Thread the right string through the beads heading the opposite way. Pull on opposite ends of the string until the beads are centered below the previous bead rows. Repeat this two more times to make the neck.

Step 5

Place 4 green beads on the left string. Thread the right string through the beads in the opposite direction. Pull on opposite ends until the beads are centered. Then place 4 yellow beads on the left string. Loop around the last 3 beads, and place the left string through the first yellow bead. Hold the beads with your left hand, pulling the tail end of the left string with your right hand until the yellow beads are flush with the row of 4 green beads.

Check-In!

At this point, your project should have a head, small neck, and the start of a body with one yellow spine. This is a hard pattern, and will take a while. Grab some water if you need, and make sure your project looks similar to the image on the right before continuing.

Step 6

Place 4 green beads on the right string. Using the same technique shown in Step 5, loop around the last 3 beads before placing the string back through the first green bead and pulling until the limb is flush with the body. Then, place 5 green beads onto the left string, and thread the right string through in the opposite direction. Pull on the tail ends of the thread until the row of 5 is centered below the previous row of 4 green beads.

Step 7

Repeat the process shown in Steps 5 and 6. Do this TWO TIMES (or more) to create the main body of your stegosaurus.

Important to note: do not do the section of Steps 5 and 6 with the 4 green beads to create extra legs. Focus on creating spines using yellow beads, and body rows using green beads.

Step 8

Place 4 green beads on the left string, and thread the right string through in the opposite direction. Pull on opposite ends of string until the row is centered beneath the main body. Then, use the technique detailed in Step 6 to create another leg for the stegosaurus.

Step 9

Take the string on the left, and place 3 yellow beads on it. Thread it through the leftmost green bead of the bottom row, looping around so the string comes out to the left. This creates the final spine!

Step 10

Place 2 green beads on the left string. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the stegosaurus's body.

Repeat this until your tail is at a desired length!

All Done!

You are all done! Congrats on finishing the pattern. Now that you've finished, your stegosaurus should look like the one on the right. If you've gotten the pattern down expertly, you can always try to make it again in a different color!

Since you've surely by now mastered the website's patterns, go try a random pattern online! And don't forget to have fun :)
