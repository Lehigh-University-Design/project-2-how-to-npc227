Simple Lizard

Step 1

Take two strings of different color, both roughly forearm length. Tie their ends together, leaving two loose ends of seperate colors to work with. Lay it out flat in front of you.

Step 2

Place 2 blue beads on the left string. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the initial knot.

Step 3

Eye time! Take the left string, place one yellow bead, one blue, and one more yellow bead on it. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the initial knot.

Step 4

Place 2 blue beads on the left string. Thread the right string through all beads heading the opposite way. Grab both ends of string and pull until all beads are centered under the initial knot.

Step 5

Leg day! Thread one blue then one purple bead on the left string. Loop the string around the purple bead, thread it back through the blue bead. Then hold the beads in your left hand, using your right hand to pull the tail end of the string towards the bead animal's body until it's leg is flush with the first row. Repeat for the right string.

Step 6

Thread two blue beads on the left string, and center them using the method described in Step 2. Then place one blue, one green, and another blue on the left string. Thread the right string through the blue, green, blue beads headed the opposite way. Grab both ends and pull in opposite directions. Repeat two more times to make the main body (a total of 3 rows with green middle beads).

Step 7

Leg day! Thread one blue then one purple bead on the left string. Loop the string around the purple bead, thread it back through the blue bead. Then hold the beads in your left hand, using your right hand to pull the tail end of the string towards the bead animal's body until it's leg is flush with the first row. Repeat for the right string.

Step 8

Place a singular blue bead on the left string. Thread the right string through going the bead heading the opposite way as the left string's tail end. Pull in opposite directions. Repeat this a minimum of 2 times to create the tail of your lizard.

All Done!

You are all done! Congrats on finishing the pattern. Now that you've finished, your bead lizard should look like the one on the right. If you've gotten the pattern down expertly, you can always try to make it again in a different color or try a harder pattern!
